Invento —
Inventory Desk

Electron Offline-first SQLite Supabase Sync Multi-warehouse

A desktop inventory management app built for small teams. Manage products, orders, suppliers, and warehouses — all stored locally so it works without an internet connection, with automatic background sync to the cloud when you're online.

Features
📦 Products

Add products with images, SKUs, categories, unit pricing, and stock levels. Quickly search and filter your entire catalogue.

🏭 Multi-Warehouse

Track inventory across multiple locations. View per-warehouse stock levels and transfer stock between sites.

📋 Purchase Orders

Create purchase orders, receive goods from suppliers, and track delivery status at every stage.

🚚 Sales Orders

Create and ship sales orders. Auto-generate PDF invoices with embedded QR codes for each order.

👥 Suppliers & Customers

Manage full contact details for suppliers and customers with dedicated detail panels and order history.

📊 Reports

Inventory value, movement history, and dead stock reports. Export any report to PDF or Excel in one click.

🔔 Stock Alerts

Automatic low-stock and out-of-stock notifications so you never miss a reorder point.

📄 Audit Trail

Every stock movement — receipts, shipments, transfers, and adjustments — is logged with a full history.

❒ Barcode & QR Codes

Generate and download barcodes and QR codes for any product. Print-ready for shelving and shipping labels.

☁ Cloud Sync

Writes go to local SQLite instantly. A background engine syncs changes to Supabase within 30 seconds when online.

🔒 Role-Based Access

Four roles — Admin, Manager, Staff, and Viewer — with fine-grained permissions on create, edit, delete, and user management.

How it works
◆ Offline-first writes. Every action writes to a local SQLite database first — the UI responds instantly with no waiting on network calls.
◆ Background sync. A sync engine queues changes and pushes them to Supabase (Postgres) in the background, typically within 30 seconds of coming online.
◆ Conflict resolution. On pull, remote state wins for any row not in the local sync queue — ensuring consistency across devices without data loss.
◆ Local images. Product images are stored in userData/product-images/ and served via a custom local-image:// protocol — no external hosting needed.
